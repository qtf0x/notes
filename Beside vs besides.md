**Beside**: next to, in comparison to
**Besides**: except for, aside from, in addition to

A nice eggcorn is found in *beside the point* and *besides the point*. Following directly from the definitions, one might expect the latter to be correct; an idea may be irrelevant if it is aside from or in addition to the point being discussed. However, the idiomatic phrase is, in fact, *beside the point*. Here we are not using “point” to mean an idea, but rather to mean a mark, as at the start of a race. If you are beside the point, you are not on it — you’ve missed the mark.
