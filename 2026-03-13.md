My only real goal in life is to be better than my father. I want to amount to something, to have some internal purpose that drives me forward into each day, something more than just survival. If I can avoid becoming him, avoid the oafish, masculine, disgusting, inconsiderate, incurious excuse for a role model that so much of him represents, then I will have succeeded. Indeed, it seems my great struggle in life will be extracting what good I can from my parents, peeling apart their mess, deciding what to keep and how to throw away the rest. There are good qualities in my father: occasional kindness, selflessness, skepticism, and work ethic. If I can find these in myself, and yet otherwise become the perfect opposite of him, then maybe I’ll somewhat resemble the person I’m meant to be.
